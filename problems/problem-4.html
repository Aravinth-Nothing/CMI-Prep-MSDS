Problem 3

Prove that, $$\frac{\sec8\theta-1}{\sec4\theta-1}=\frac{\tan8\theta}{\tan2\theta}$$

Solution:

$$\frac{\sec8\theta-1}{\sec4\theta-1}=\frac{\frac{1}{\cos8\theta}-1}{\frac{1}{\cos4\theta}-1}=\frac{\frac{1-\cos8\theta}{\cos8\theta}}{\frac{1-\cos4\theta}{\cos4\theta}}$$ $$=\frac{\frac{1-(1-2\sin^24\theta)}{\cos8\theta}}{\frac{1-(1-2\sin^22\theta)}{\cos4\theta}}=\frac{2\sin^24\theta \cos4\theta}{2\cos8\theta \sin^22\theta}$$ $$=\frac{2\sin4\theta \cos4\theta.\sin4\theta}{2\sin^22\theta\cos8\theta}=\frac{\sin8\theta \sin4\theta}{2\sin^2\theta\cos8\theta}$$ $$=\frac{\sin8\theta.\cancel{2\sin2\theta} \cos2\theta}{\cancel{2}\sin^\cancel{2}2\theta \cos8\theta}=\frac{\sin8\theta \cos2\theta}{\sin2\theta \cos8\theta}$$ $$=\frac{\frac{\sin8\theta}{\cos8\theta}}{\frac{\sin2\theta}{\cos2\theta}}=\frac{\tan8\theta}{\tan2\theta}$$

Hence Proved.
