Problem 12

Let $f:(0,1)\to\mathbb{R}$ be defined by $f(x)=\frac{b-x}{1-bx}$ where b is a constant
where b is a constant such that, $0\lt b \lt 1$. Then,
a) $f$ is not invertible on $(0,1)$
b) $f \neq f^{-1}$ on $(0,1)$ and $f'(b)=\frac{1}{f'(0)}$
c) $f = f^{-1}$ on $(0,1)$ and $f'(b)=\frac{1}{f'(0)}$
d) $f^{-1}$ is differentiable on $(0,1)$

Solution

Given the function, $$f(x)=\frac{b-x}{1-bx}$$ To check if $f(x)$ is invertible it should be one-one and onto.
One-one check: Differentiating $f(x)$ $$f'(x)=\frac{b^2-1}{(1-bx)^2}$$ $f'(x)$ is always negative $\therefore$ it is one-one.
Onto check:
For a Domain of $\mathbb{R}$, find range of $f(x),$
Invert the function and find its range,
Inverse is, $$f^{-1}(x)=\frac{b-x}{1-bx}$$ Domain of $f^{-1}(x)$ is $\mathbb{R}$ is $\mathbb{R}-{\frac{1}{b}}$, which is the range of $f(x)$.
Co-Domain of $f(x)$ is $\mathbb{R}$
Range of $f(x)$ for a Domain of $\mathbb{R}$ is $\mathbb{R}-{\frac{1}{b}}$
There for no value of $x$ in $(0,1)$ can give, $\frac{1}{b}$.
Co-Domain $\neq$ Range
$f(x)$ is not Onto.
$f(x)$ is not invertible.

a) $f$ is not invertible on $(0,1)$
