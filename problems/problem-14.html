Problem 14

$\displaystyle \lim_{x\to \frac{\pi}{4}} \frac{\int_2^{\sec^2x} f(t)dt}{x^2-\frac{\pi^2}{16}}$ equals to,
a) $\frac{8}{\pi}f(2)$
a) $\frac{2}{\pi}f(2)$
a) $\frac{2}{\pi}f(\frac{1}{2})$
a) $4f(2)$

Solution

Note:
Lebinitz rule:
$$\frac{d}{dx}\int_{h(x)}^{g(x)} f(x)dx=f(g(x)).g'(x)-f(h(x)).h'(x)$$ We use L'Hospital's rule and differentiate, numerator and denominator. $$\displaystyle \lim_{x\to \frac{\pi}{4}}\frac{\int_2^{\sec^2x} f(t)dt}{x^2-\frac{\pi^2}{16}}=\displaystyle \lim_{x\to \frac{\pi}{4}}\frac{f(\sec^2x).2\sec x.\sec x\tan x -f(2).(0)}{2x}$$ Apply $x=\frac{\pi}{4}$ in the limit, $$$$ $$=\displaystyle \lim_{x\to \frac{\pi}{4}}\frac{f(\sec^2\frac{\pi}{4}).2\sec \frac{\pi}{4}.\sec \frac{\pi}{4}\tan \frac{\pi}{4} -f(2).(0)}{2\frac{\pi}{4}}$$ Note: $\sec\frac{\pi}{4}=\sqrt{2},\tan\frac{\pi}{4}=1$ $$=\frac{f(2).2(\sqrt{2}).(\sqrt{2}).(1)}{\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right)}=\frac{8f(2)}{\pi}$$

a) $\frac{8}{\pi}f(2)$ is Correct.
