Problem 9

If $\frac{x}{\cos\theta}=\frac{y}{\cos(\theta-\frac{2\pi}{3})}=\frac{z}{\cos(\theta+\frac{2\pi}{3})},$

then what is the value of $x+y+z$?

Solution

Let $$\frac{x}{\cos\theta}=\frac{y}{\cos(\theta-\frac{2\pi}{3})}=\frac{z}{\cos(\theta+\frac{2\pi}{3})}=k$$ $$x=k\cos\theta\label{one}\tag{1}$$ To find $y$, $$\cos(\theta-\frac{2\pi}{3})=\cos\theta \cos \frac{2\pi}{3}+\sin\theta \sin\frac{2\pi}{3}$$ Note: $\cos(\pi-x)=-\cos x$,
$cos\frac{2\pi}{3}=\cos(\pi-\frac{\pi}{3})=-\cos\frac{\pi}{3}=-\frac{1}{2}$
$\sin\frac{2\pi}{3}=\sqrt{1-(\frac{-1}{2})^2}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ $$\cos(\theta-\frac{2\pi}{3})=-\frac{\cos\theta}{2}+\frac{\sqrt{3}\sin\theta}{2}$$ $$\therefore y=-\frac{k\cos\theta}{2}+\frac{k\sqrt{3}\sin\theta}{2}$$ Similarly, $$z=-\frac{k\cos\theta}{2}-\frac{k\sqrt{3}\sin\theta}{2}$$ $$x+y+z=\cancel{k\cos\theta}-\cancel{\frac{k\cos\theta}{2}}+\cancel{\frac{k\sqrt{3}\sin\theta}{2}}-\cancel{\frac{k\cos\theta}{2}}-\cancel{\frac{k\sqrt{3}\sin\theta}{2}}$$ $$\therefore x+y+z=0$$
