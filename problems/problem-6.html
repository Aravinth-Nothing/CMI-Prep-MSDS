Problem 6

Show that $\frac{1}{\sin10^\circ}-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{\cos10^\circ}=4$

Solution

Take the left hand side of the equation. $$\frac{1}{\sin10^\circ}-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{\cos10^\circ}$$

Multiply and divide by 2.
$$2.\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{\sin10^\circ}-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{\cos10^\circ}\right)=2\left(\frac{1}{2\sin10^\circ}-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2\cos10^\circ}\right)$$ $$=2\left(\frac{\sin30^\circ}{\sin10^\circ}-\frac{\cos30^\circ}{\cos10^\circ}\right)=2\left(\frac{\cos10^\circ\sin30^\circ-\cos30^\circ\sin10^\circ}{\sin10^\circ\cos10^\circ}\right)$$ $$=2\left(\frac{\sin(30^\circ-10^\circ)}{\sin10^\circ\cos10^\circ}\right)$$
Multiptly and Divide by 2.
$$2\left(2\left(\frac{\sin20^\circ}{2\sin10^\circ \cos10^\circ}\right)\right)=4\left(\frac{\sin20^\circ}{\sin20^\circ}\right)=4$$
Hence Shown.
