Problem 13

Let $f(x)=\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin(x)\right)\right)\forall x\in \mathbb{R}$ and $g(x)=\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x \forall x \in \mathbb{R}$
Let $(f \circ g)(x)$ denote $f(g(x))$ and $(g \circ f)(x)$ denote $g(f(x))$ then which
of the following is/are true?
a) Range of $f$ is $\left[-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}\right]$
b) Range of $f \circ g$ is $\left[-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}\right]$
c) $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}=\frac{\pi}{6}$
d) There is an $x\in\mathbb{R}$ such that $(g\circ f)(x)=1$

Solution

Let's check options one by one:
a) Range of $f$ is $\left[-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}\right]$

Range of $\sin x \to [-1,1]$
Range of $\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x \to [-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2}]$
Range of $\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x\right) \to [-1,1]$
Range of $\frac{\pi}{6}\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x\right) \to [-\frac{\pi}{6},\frac{\pi}{6}]$
Range of $\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x\right)\right) \to [-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}]$
a) Range of $f$ is $\left[-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}\right]$ is correct.

b) Range of $f \circ g$ is $\left[-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}\right]$
$f \circ g = f(g(x)) = \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x\right)\right)\right)$
Range of $\sin x \to [-1,1]$
Range of $\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x \to [-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2}]$
Range of $\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x\right) \to [-1,1]$
Range of $\frac{\pi}{2}\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x\right) \to [-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2}]$
Range of $\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x\right)\right) \to [-1,1]$
Range of $\frac{\pi}{6}\left(\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x\right)\right)\right) \to [-\frac{\pi}{6},\frac{\pi}{6}]$
Range of $\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\left(\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x\right)\right)\right)\right) \to [-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}]$
b) Range of $f \circ g$ is $\left[-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}\right]$ is correct.

c) $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}=\frac{\pi}{6}$
$\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}=\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0} \frac{\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin(x)\right)\right)}{\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x}$
$\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0}\frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}=\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0}\frac{\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\left(\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x\right)\right)\right . \frac{\pi}{6}\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin x\right)).\frac{\pi}{2}\cos x}{\frac{\pi}{2}\cos x}$
$\frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}=\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0}\frac{\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\left(\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin 0\right)\right)\right . \frac{\pi}{6}\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\sin 0\right)).\frac{\pi}{2}\cos 0}{\frac{\pi}{2}\cos 0}$
$=\cos(0).\frac{\pi}{6}.\cos(0)$

c)$\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}=\frac{\pi}{6}$ is correct.

d) There is an $x\in\mathbb{R}$ such that $(g\circ f)(x)=1$
$(g\circ f)=g(f(x))$
Range of $g(f(x)) \to g($Range of $f(x))\to g\left(\left[-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}\right]\right)\to\frac{\pi}{2}\sin\left(\left[-\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2}\right]\right)$
Range of $g(f(x))\to\left[-\frac{\pi}{2}\sin\left(\frac{1}{2}\right),\frac{\pi}{2}\sin\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)\right]\to (-0.75,0.75)$
$\therefore g \circ f \neq 1 \forall x$
d) There is an $x\in\mathbb{R}$ such that $(g\circ f)(x)=1$ is wrong.

(a),(b),(c) is Correct.
