Problem 20

Let the function $g:(-\infty,\infty)\to(-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2})$ be given by $g(u)=2\tan^{-1}(e^u)-\frac{\pi}{2}$.
Then, $g$ is,
a) even and is strictly increasing in $(0,\infty)$
b) odd and is strictly decreasing in $(-\infty,\infty)$
c) odd and is strictly increasing in $(-\infty,\infty)$
d) neither even nor odd, but is strictly increasing in $(-\infty,\infty)$

Solution

Checking Odd or Even,
$$g(-u)=2\tan^{-1}(e^{-u})-\frac{\pi}{2}=2\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{e^u}\right)-\frac{\pi}{2}$$ $$=2\cot^{-1}(e^u)-\frac{\pi}{2}$$ $$g(u)+g(-u)=2\tan^{-1}(e^u)-\frac{\pi}{2}+2\cot^{-1}(e^u)-\frac{\pi}{2}$$ Note: $\tan^{-1}x+\cot^{-1}x=\frac{\pi}{2}$ $$=2(\tan^{-1}(e^u)+\cot^{-1}(e^u))-\pi=2(\frac{\pi}{2})-\pi=0$$ $$g(u)+g(-u)=0$$ $$g(-u)=-g(u)$$

$g(u)$ is an odd function.

$u$ is an increasing function.
$e^u$ is an increasing function.
$\tan^{-1}(e^u)$ is an increasing function.
$2\tan^{-1}(e^u)$ is an increasing function.
$2\tan^{-1}(e^u)-\frac{\pi}{2}$ is an increasing function.
$\therefore g(u)$ is an odd and is strictly increasing in $(-\infty,\infty)$
c) is the correct Answer.
