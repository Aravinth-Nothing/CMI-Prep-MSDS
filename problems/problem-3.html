Problem 3

If $\frac{\cos^4A}{\cos^2B}+\frac{\sin^4A}{\sin^2B}=1$, then prove that
  a) $\sin^4A+\sin^4B=2\sin^2A\sin^2B$   b) $\frac{\cos^4B}{\cos^2A}+\frac{\sin^4B}{\sin^2A}=1$

Solution:

Given, $$\frac{\cos^4A}{cos^2B}+\frac{\sin^4A}{sin^2B}=1$$ We know that, $${\cos^x=1-\sin^2x}$$ Substituting, $$\frac{(1-\sin^2A)^2}{1-\sin^2B}+\frac{\sin^4A}{\sin^2B}=1$$ $$\frac{\sin^2B(1+\sin^4A-2\sin^2A)}{\sin^2B-\sin^4B}+\frac{\sin^4A-\sin^2B\sin^4A}{\sin^2B-\sin^4B}=1$$ $$\frac{\sin^2B+\cancel{\sin^2B\sin^4A}-2\sin^2A\sin^2B+\sin^4A-\cancel{\sin^2B\sin^4A}}{\sin^2B-\sin^4B}=1$$ $$\cancel{\sin^2B}-2\sin^2A\sin^2B+\sin^4A=\cancel{\sin^2B}-\sin^4B$$ $$\sin^4A+\sin^4B=2\sin^2A\sin^2B \label{one} \tag{1} $$

a) Hence Proved.
From \eqref{one} $$\sin^4A+\sin^4B=2\sin^2A\sin^2B=0$$ $$(\sin^2A-\sin^2B)^2=0$$ $$\therefore \sin^2A=\sin^2B\label{two} \tag{2}$$ $$1-\cos^2A=1-\cos^2B$$ $$\cos^2A=\cos^2B\label{three} \tag{3}$$ Substituting \eqref{two} and \eqref{three} in the original equation, $\frac{\cos^4A}{\cos^2B}+\frac{\sin^4A}{\sin^2B}=1$ $$\frac{(\cos^2A)^2}{\cos^2B}+\frac{(\sin^2A)^2}{\sin^2B}=1$$ $$\frac{\cos^4B}{\cos^2A}+\frac{\sin^4B}{\sin^2A}=1\label{four} \tag{4}$$
b) Hence Proved.
