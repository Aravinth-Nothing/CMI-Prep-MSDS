Problem 5

What is the value of $(1+\cos\frac{\pi}{8})(1+\cos\frac{3\pi}{8})(1+\cos\frac{5\pi}{8})(1+\cos\frac{7\pi}{8})$

Solution

$$=(1+\cos\frac{\pi}{8})(1+\cos(\frac{\pi}{2}-\frac{\pi}{8}))(1+\cos(\frac{\pi}{2}+\frac{\pi}{8}))(1+\cos(\pi-\frac{\pi}{8}))$$ $$=(1+\cos\frac{\pi}{8})(1+\sin\frac{\pi}{8})(1-\sin\frac{\pi}{8})(1-\cos\frac{\pi}{8})$$ $$=(1-\cos^2\frac{\pi}{8})(1-\sin^2\frac{\pi}{8})$$ $$=(\sin^2\frac{\pi}{8})(\cos^2\frac{\pi}{8})$$

Multiply and Divide by 4.
$$=\frac{1}{4}(4)(\sin^2\frac{\pi}{8})(\cos^2\frac{\pi}{8})=\frac{1}{4}(2\sin\frac{\pi}{8} \cos\frac{\pi}{8})^2$$ $$=\frac{1}{4}(\sin(2*(\frac{\pi}{8})))^2=\frac{1}{4}(\sin\frac{\pi}{4})^2$$ $$\frac{1}{4}*(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}})^2=\frac{1}{8}$$
